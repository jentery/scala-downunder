Scala Downunder 2016 - Call For Papers

Scala Downunder 2016 CFP is now open. Once again the conference will bring some functional programming magic to the Southern Hemisphere.

The conference goal is to empower the community by sharing our collective love and knowledge of functional programming. Attendees have a very broad mix of experience, from the seasoned developers to the very early stages of evaluation and/or adoption, hence we're looking for a good mix of topics, frameworks and levels.

The first day will be single track and all sessions will be recorded.

The second day will be less formal with workshops and pairing sessions. Depending on submissions we'll add more talks.

We're encouraging as many people to speak as possible and here are some ideas:

  • Getting started with FP, or any specific FP language, e.g. Scala, Clojure, Haskell, F#, Erlang, Swift, OCaml, Idris, Elm, etc.
  • Tips and tricks
  • Functional Programming
  • Frameworks, e.g. Play, Yesod, Pedestal or Datomic
  • FP performance / scalability
  • Apache Spark
  • Functional Reactive Programming (FRP) and Reactive ... anything/everything
  • Distributed Systems
  • Build systems
  • Type systems

In order to submit your interest in speaking at the 2016 conference please complete the form by 31 October 2015.

Should you be successful we will contact you in November to confirm your participation as a speaker at Scala Downunder 2016.
