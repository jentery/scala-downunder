Welcome

Typesafe and Movio brought you the first ever Scala Downunder Conference that was held at Movio HQ on September 5 – 6 2014 in Auckland, NZ.

This conference brought together the Scala Australasian community with the first day offering a program of keynote presentations, expert guest speakers, and informal networking with fellow developers. The second day was more hands on, with workshops for both beginner and advanced Scala developers, pairing sessions and Scala Days video replays.

Due to the success of the inaugural event we will return in 2015, so watch this space for updates.

Scala Downunder is a unique opportunity to share your experiences and new ideas around creating applications with Scala, and related Typesafe technologies.

Watch Keynote speaker Jonas Bonér, CTO & Co-Founder of Typesafe - “A 5 year retrospective and the future of distributed computing”.

              Reactive Streams and Play 3.0
              In person, by
              • James Roper (@jroper)
                Senior Software Engineer at Typesafe

                James is an Australian open source developer working as the lead developer for Play Framework at Typesafe. His passion is to see reactive software development adopted in the enterprise. James has a wide range of experience ranging from web scale social networks to working on Atlassian’s SaaS services to manufacturing control systems.

              Reactive Streams has a lot to offer Play, providing an asynchronous streaming that is easy to use from both Java and Scala, and a common API to share between asynchronous streaming libraries on the JVM. Play 3.0 will be embracing this new standard as a base for its IO streaming, but what implications will this have on Play’s architecture? What will happen to iteratees? How much will this impact existing Play applications? This presentation will dive deep into Play’s architecture, and show just how reactive streams will be applied to and improve Play.

                Controlling computers and other electronic devices using the power of thought is definitely not science fiction, thanks to brain-computer interface technology (BCI). This field is rapidly developing, in both research labs and the commercial market. But the field is still heavily focused on technological development, often ignoring the human user in the equation. This presentation will provide the audience with a glimpse of my experiences in developing a BCI assistive solution for individuals with severe physical impairment, highlighting the importance of implementing a user-centered approach. There will also be a live BCI demonstration.

            Code of Conduct

            Our Code of Conduct aligns with Scala Days.

            Nobody likes a jerk, so please show respect for those around you. This applies to both in-person and online behavior.

            Scala Downunder is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion (or lack thereof). We do not tolerate harassment of participants in any form.

            All communication should be appropriate for a technical audience, including people of many different backgrounds. Sexual language, innuendo, and imagery is not appropriate for any conference venue, including talks.

            Participants violating these rules may be asked to leave without a refund at the sole discretion of the organizers.
