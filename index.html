@ScalaDownunder|5 - 6 September 2014, Auckland, New Zealand

Welcome

Typesafe and Movio are excited to bring you the first ever Scala Downunder Conference, September 5 – 6 in Auckland, NZ.

Bringing together the Scala Australasian community, the first day of Scala Downunder will offer a program of keynote presentations, expert guest speakers, and informal networking with fellow developers. The second day will be more hands on, with workshops for both beginner and advanced Scala developers, pairing sessions and Scala Days video replays. This is a unique opportunity to share your experiences and new ideas around creating applications with Scala, and related Typesafe technologies.

Places are strictly limited for this inaugural conference, so make sure you register to attend before Friday 15 August 2014 to avoid disappointment.

Cost per person:

$165 NZD

Includes all conference material, event T-shirt, and food and beverages during the conference. Accommodation, travel and additional meals are at the cost of each attendee.

                Brain-computer Interface Technology and User Centered Design
                In person, by

                  Researcher at Thought-Wired

                  A reformed luddite, PhD candidate in Psychology and UX/Usability researcher at Thought-Wired, a start-up neurotech company with a strong focus on social impact. We’re developing a software assistive solution for individuals with severe physical impairments that’s controlled by the electrical activity of the brain.

                Controlling computers and other electronic devices using the power of thought is definitely not science fiction, thanks to brain-computer interface technology (BCI). This field is rapidly developing, in both research labs and the commercial market. But the field is still heavily focused on technological development, often ignoring the human user in the equation. This presentation will provide the audience with a glimpse of my experiences in developing a BCI assistive solution for individuals with severe physical impairment, highlighting the importance of implementing a user-centered approach. There will also be a live BCI demonstration.
