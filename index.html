Welcome

Typesafe and Movio brought you the first ever Scala Downunder Conference that was held at Movio HQ, Auckland on 5 - 6 September 2014.

The 2016 conference is broader to include all Functional Programming (FP), welcoming other FP communities like: Clojure, Haskell, F#, Erlang, Swift, OCaml, Idris, Elm, etc.

Mark up your calendar for the next edition – 19-20 February 2016!

Scala Downunder brings together the FP Australasian community with the first day offering a program of keynote presentations, expert guest speakers, and informal networking with fellow developers. The second day is more hands on, with workshops for both beginner and advanced FP developers, and pairing sessions.

Scala Downunder is a unique opportunity to share your experiences and new ideas around creating software with FP techniques.

Call for Papers

Scala Downunder 2016 - Call For Papers

Scala Downunder 2016 CFP is now open. Once again the conference will bring some functional programming magic to the Southern Hemisphere.

The conference goal is to empower the community by sharing our collective love and knowledge of functional programming. Attendees have a very broad mix of experience, from the seasoned developers to the very early stages of evaluation and/or adoption, hence we're looking for a good mix of topics, frameworks and levels.

The first day will be single track and all sessions will be recorded.

The second day will be less formal with workshops and pairing sessions. Depending on submissions we'll add more talks.

We're encouraging as many people to speak as possible and here are some ideas:

  • Getting started with FP, or any specific FP language, e.g. Scala, Clojure, Haskell, F#, Erlang, Swift, OCaml, Idris, Elm, etc.
  • Tips and tricks
  • Functional Programming
  • Frameworks, e.g. Play, Yesod, Pedestal or Datomic
  • FP performance / scalability
  • Apache Spark
  • Functional Reactive Programming (FRP) and Reactive ... anything/everything
  • Distributed Systems
  • Build systems
  • Type systems

In order to submit your interest in speaking at the 2016 conference please complete the form by 31 October 2015.

Should you be successful we will contact you in November to confirm your participation as a speaker at Scala Downunder 2016.

Code of Conduct

Our Code of Conduct aligns with Scala Days.

Nobody likes a jerk, so please show respect for those around you. This applies to both in-person and online behaviour.

Scala Downunder is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion (or lack thereof). We do not tolerate harassment of participants in any form.

All communication should be appropriate for a technical audience, including people of many different backgrounds. Sexual language, innuendo, and imagery is not appropriate for any conference venue, including talks.

Participants violating these rules may be asked to leave without a refund at the sole discretion of the organisers.
